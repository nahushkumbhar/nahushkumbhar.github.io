Thesis, Projects and Internships

MSc Thesis: Improving the Sustainability of Large Infrastructure
using Passive Cooling Techniques

My research for the MSc was focused on identification of Phase Change Materials (PCM) and heat exchange techniques for passive cooling applications. The aim was to devise a technique to reduce the cooling load on a heat pump system used for cooling transmission electron microscopes. Part of research was to come up with a design of heat exchanger and PCM arrangement that can be implemented first at pilot scale.

Challenges:

  • Identification of PCM whch could function within a very thin temperature margin (17 to 23 deg C).
  • Select an effective heat exchange option with less space consumption.
  • Simulate the heat exchange across pipe crossections to identify the effectivity of the implementation of the system.
  • Identify the possible Carbon savings using extrapolation.

    • Results

    Designed the system that can save up to 47 KWh and 14 kg CO2 per day. Estimated the potential CO2 and KWh Savings which included the analysis of energy consumption of the existing system. Built an Ansys fluent simulation model for heat transfer analysis.

    UG Thesis: Designing Production System for Oil Well

    Research work in artificial lift systems used to pump oil from the well bores with depeleted pressure or insufficient pressure to overcome the liquid head. My project included calculations, analysis for the best possible lift technique and a short comparison of the diffrent artificial lift techniques.

    Challenges:

  • Filtration and analysis of historic production data.
  • Detection of the best artificial lift technique that can be utilised considering the existing pressure, temperture and physical conditions.

    • Results:

    Calculated design properties and pump characteristics for an ESP production system for a vertical oil well. Used MS Excel for data filtration, Pump head, and flow calculations. Calculated the multiphase flow rates of the well fluids using 'PROSPER', a simulation software.

    Assessed Project in Reservoir Engineering

    This was a semester project. Works include, evaluation of the oil reservoir properties and well performance analysis using history match technique on software 'ECRIN'. Pressure Transient Analysis to determine oil reservoir properties. Aim of the analysis was to calculate skin factor and permeability of the reservoir and study the behavior of the Pressure transient.

    Project Intern: Oil and Natural Gas Corporation, Mumbai

    Summer research internship at the India's crown Petroleum Organization, ONGC. Research work was on natural gas hydrate formation in gas pipelines. As a part, I Engineered solutions to mitigate hydrate formation in a high-pressure gas pipeline using chemical methods. Simulated gas hydrate formation using simulation software 'PIPESIM' to study the hydrate behavior. One of the focus point of research was to study the Gas dehydration techniques used at ONGC. The aim of this research was to extract a hydrate mitigation technique for an ongoing ONGC pipeline project at Assam, India as a part of pre design analysis.
